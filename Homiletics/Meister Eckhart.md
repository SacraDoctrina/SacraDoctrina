# Meister Eckhart

The Complete Work of Meister Eckhart

## SERMON EIGHTY-SEVEN

(Pf 87, Q 52, QT 32)

BEAT! PAUPERES SPIRITU QUIA IPSORUM EST REGNUM CAELORUM (Mattew 5:3)

a poor man is one who wants nothing, knows nothing, and has nothing.

Firstly, we say that a poor man is one who wants nothing.  
Therefore let us pray to God that we may be free of God that we may gain the truth and enjoy it eternally, there where the highest angel, the fly, and the soul are equal, there where I stood10 and wanted what I was, and was what I wanted. We conclude, then: if a man is to be poor of will, he must will and desire as little as he willed and desired when he was not. And this is the way for a man to be poor by not wanting.

Secondy, he is a poor man who knows nothing.  
For when that man stood in the eternal being of God, nothing else lived in him: what lived there was himself.  
The Proper work of man is to love and to know. ... for there is something in the soul from which both knowledge and love flow: 14 but it does not itself know or love in the way the powers of the soul do. ... It is in this manner, I declare, that a man should be so acquitted and free that he neither knows nor realizes that God is at work in him: in that way can a man possess poverty.

Thirdly, he is a poor man who has nothing.
